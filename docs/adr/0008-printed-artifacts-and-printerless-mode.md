### Printed artifacts catalog and printerless-mode handling

Vajra prints exactly two kinds of artifact at the counter — a **Sale Invoice** and a **Credit Voucher** — and only at the moments those entities are finished. Every Sale produces a Sale Invoice with **two copies by default** (business + customer); the cashier may **opt out** of the extra copy during the Sale before finish. A Credit Sale additionally produces a Credit Voucher that the customer signs and the shopkeeper keeps. Invoice and voucher for a Credit Sale both show the **same transaction ID** (ADR-0009). No other transaction type prints: Purchases, Receipts, Payments, Expenses, Income, and Stock Transfers commit silently and surface only through the live UI and the End of Day Report. The End of Day Report itself is an XLSX file (per ADR-0006), not a counter slip.

The Sale Invoice includes **place** and **phone** when known (from Customer Master or walk-in capture), line items, totals (including Sale **Discount** when present), payment captured on Cash Sales, and optional **remarks**.

A finished transaction is committed _after_ the printer driver acknowledges the print job, not before — so a driver-side failure leaves no orphan row, and a driver-side success leaves a recoverable record even if paper jams downstream. The Sale Invoice is reprintable from the cashier UI only until the next transaction starts; the Credit Voucher is never reprintable because the customer's signature is what makes it valid. When a single transaction encounters a transient print failure, Vajra prompts _"printer not responding — continue without slip?"_ and proceeds for that one transaction only. For sustained printer-unavailability, the shopkeeper toggles **Printerless Mode** in settings; Vajra then commits without printing and instead displays the would-be Sale Invoice and Credit Voucher details on screen, including the transaction ID, so the cashier can write manual copies by hand.

We chose this narrow print surface because the goods-moving moments (Sale, Credit Sale) are the only ones where a third party — the customer — needs paper to walk away with. Payments, Receipts, and Expenses already produce their own paper artifacts in the real world (a supplier's bill, an electricity board's receipt, a customer's note). Printing Vajra-side duplicates of those would clutter the workflow without adding evidence. The two-mode print-failure handling — per-transaction prompt vs settings-level toggle — keeps a temporary jam from accidentally committing the cashier to manual writing forever, while still giving a clean operating mode for when the printer is genuinely out of action for the day.

#### Consequences

- Telugu-first rendering with English fallback (ADR-0003) applies to both the Sale Invoice and the Credit Voucher for customer name, place, and product line names. Missing Telugu does not leave a blank handwriting gap.
- Printerless Mode and the per-transaction skip prompt must both display the transaction ID, since the cashier may need to handwrite it on a manual copy and the shopkeeper will reconcile by these IDs.
- Adding a new transaction type later (or deciding Purchases should print after all) means updating this ADR — the current catalog is intentionally closed, not a default-on shape.
- The "next transaction starts" boundary for Sale Invoice reprinting must be defined explicitly in code (cart-open? finish-clicked? first-line-added?) — chosen to match the cashier's mental model of "I'm done with the last one."
- Opt-out of the second invoice copy is a per-Sale control at finish time, defaulting to two copies on.
